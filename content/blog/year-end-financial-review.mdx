December is the only month where several financial levers are still within reach before the tax year locks. Most people let it pass without taking stock. A structured year-end review takes two to three hours and can meaningfully improve your financial position heading into the new year.

## Review Your Tax Situation

Start here because the deadline is fixed. In Norway, contributions to IPS (individual pension savings) must be made before December 31 to qualify for the current year's deduction. The same applies to charitable donations that qualify for fradrag. If you've had capital gains during the year, check whether you have unrealised losses you could realise before year-end to offset them — this is called tax-loss harvesting and is entirely legal.

Check whether your income this year has pushed you into a different bracket or affected your trygdeavgift or trinnskatt calculation. If you have significant dividend income or freelance earnings, make sure your advance tax payments are sufficient to avoid a large settlement bill in April.

## Assess Your Savings Rate

Pull together your income and actual spending for the year. Not estimates — actuals. What percentage of your take-home pay did you save? Most financial planners suggest 15–20% as a minimum for long-term wealth building, but the right number depends on your age, obligations, and goals.

If your savings rate fell short of target, identify specifically where the gap came from. A single large unexpected expense is different from a structural overspend in a recurring category. One requires an emergency fund review; the other requires a budget adjustment.

## Rebalance Your Portfolio

Markets move, and over the course of a year your asset allocation drifts. A portfolio that started at 80% equities and 20% bonds may have shifted to 87/13 if equities performed well. Rebalancing restores your intended risk level and enforces a mechanical discipline of selling high and buying low.

Check each account separately — pension savings, ASK accounts, regular brokerage accounts. Note that in an ASK account, you can rebalance within the account without triggering tax, which makes it the right place to hold equity funds if you plan to trade between them.

## Review Insurance Coverage

Once a year is enough to check that your insurance coverage still matches your life. Has your income increased significantly? Your income protection coverage should reflect current earnings, not what you made three years ago. Have you acquired new assets — a car, valuables, renovation work on your home? Contents and buildings insurance limits may need updating.

Death and disability coverage is the one most people neglect. If you have dependents, check that your gjeldsforsikring and livsforsikring would actually cover what they'd need.

## Set Next Year's Goals

End with something forward-looking. Write down three to five specific financial targets for the coming year — not vague intentions but measurable commitments. "Save 150,000 NOK" is a goal. "Save more money" is not. Put them somewhere you'll see them in March when the novelty has worn off.

The review does not need to be comprehensive to be useful. Even doing half of these steps puts you ahead of most people who let the year-end pass without any reflection at all.
